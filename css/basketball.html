EARLY SPORTS

Due to me growing up in several countries I have experienced numerous sports in my life. It is only as of recent in which I chose to focus on basketball.I loved all the other sports in my life but my favourite sport still remains to be basketball

Soccer

I love soccer. I played for several years in several countries including: Kenya, Nigeria, France, and Canada.I loved soccer but had to quit the game due to a switch in country.

Football

I enjoyed football, I played wide receiver and running back but the game was too dangerous, therefore the switch to basketball.

Track

I ran track for several years. I was very good at 100m dash and a lot of short distance races. I still run track sometimes in order to stay in shape for basketball; as track is just not my passion.

Cricket

I played cricket for a brief period of time during my early years at age 8-10. I got into to cricket when I lived in Kenya as it is very popular over there

Volleyball

I played volleyball briefly during elementary school. I did not enjoy the sport so I still dont play the sport.

Field

I loved high jump, long jump, and triple jump. I would do these events in Kenya. Field is not very popular in Canada

Basketball Introduction

Basketball was introduced to me when i moved to Canada from Kenya. At first I did not enjoy the sport because I was talented in it, however I was inspired by the basketball player Lebron James. He was incredibly talented and he motivated me to be just as good as him

My first time playing organized basketball was in grade 8 when I was 13 years old. I played for my elementary school.

My first time playing basketball I wore the number 23 and played small forward en suite of Lebron James.

My first AAU team I played for was Whitby Wildcats> I started playing AAU in grade 8.

I began to follow and consistently watch the NBA in grade 9.

I began to train and take basketball seriously in grade 9.

I went to my first basketball game in Toronto in grade 11. I went to see Toronto Raptors vs Minnesota Timberwolves.

Keep your body fit & strong

Speed and agility training is crucial for basketball to improve footwork skills as well as improve cardiorespiratory stamina. Speed and agility training is also key in decreasing injury for basketball players. The following bullet points will review the rationale for speed and agility training for the sport of basketball.

  • Calf Raises.
  • 100m dash.
  • Box jumping.
  • Fast feet Drill.

Motivation psychology in basketball

A lot of things in sport depend on motivation. In career of each basketball player the motivation of achievements plays large role. “What such motivation of achievements?” – you ask. I will try to answer with one offer is an estimated behavior of the player which aims his motives at a victory and avoiding of failure.

  • Ask not what your teammates can do for you. Ask what you can do for your teammates.
  • Everything negative — pressure, challenges — are all an opportunity for me to rise.
  • Create unselfishness as the most important team attribute
  • I really get motivated when I have doubters.
